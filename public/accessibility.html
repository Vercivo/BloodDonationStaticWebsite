Accessibility Statement

Last updated: 5 September 2025

We are committed to providing a website that is accessible to the widest possible audience, regardless of technology or ability.

  • Accessibility features
  • Conformance status

1) Our commitment

Our goal is to meet or exceed the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA. We continuously improve usability for everyone, including users of assistive technologies.

2) Accessibility features

  • Semantic HTML with logical heading order.
  • Keyboard accessible navigation and controls.
  • Visible focus indicators for interactive elements.
  • Sufficient color contrast for text and UI components.
  • Alternative text for meaningful images and icons.
  • Form labels, validation messages, and ARIA attributes where appropriate.
  • Responsive design supporting various screen sizes and orientations.
  • Reduced motion preference respected for animated effects.

3) Conformance status

We aim for partial to full conformance with WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Some parts of the content may not fully conform due to third‑party components or legacy content. We are working to address any gaps.

4) Known limitations

  • Embedded third‑party widgets may not meet all accessibility criteria.
  • Older browsers may not fully support some ARIA or CSS features.
  • Occasional missing alt text on newly uploaded images (we correct as reported).
If you encounter an issue not listed here, please let us know and we will prioritize a fix.

5) Feedback & support

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of our website. If you need information in an alternative format or encounter a barrier, please contact us:

  • Email: info@blooddonate.example
  • Phone: +92 300 0000000
  • WhatsApp: Message us
  • Contact form: Send a request

We try to respond within 2 business days.

6) Browser and assistive technology compatibility

This website is designed to work with the latest versions of major browsers (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari) and with screen readers such as NVDA and VoiceOver. For the best experience, keep your browser and assistive technologies updated.

7) Technical specifications

Accessibility of our website relies on HTML, WAI‑ARIA, CSS, and JavaScript. These technologies are relied upon to conform with the accessibility standards used.

8) Assessment approach

  • Self‑evaluation using automated tools (e.g., Lighthouse, axe) and manual checks.
  • Ongoing content reviews during updates and new feature development.
  • User feedback and issue tracking to address barriers promptly.

9) Contact and escalation

If you are not satisfied with our response, we can escalate your request to our accessibility lead for review.
